Migration & Validation

PolicyPort

Expert-supervised, AI-accelerated migration of complex network and security estates between platforms — with continuous post-migration validation.

Book a migration assessment See how it works

The move to modern SASE / SD-WAN is inevitable — but porting thousands of policies, rules, and objects by hand doesn't scale. PolicyPort automates that processing and validates migration efficiency in real project time — and the outcome report proves a gapless transition.

The problem

Platform migrations are where large networks break

Every vendor expresses routing, SD-WAN, and security differently, so porting an estate means manually re-deriving thousands of rules, objects, and policies by hand. Across dozens to hundreds of sites this is slow, eye-wateringly expensive, and one transcription error away from an outage or a security hole.

And once the cutover is done, no one keeps verifying that the migrated policies still do what they were meant to.

What it migrates

Full-stack porting, in both directions

From legacy firewall and SD-WAN platforms — Cisco, Fortinet, Check Point — to a modern SASE / SD-WAN platform, or the reverse. Nothing copied blindly.

Routing

BGP, OSPF, static, and DMVPN / overlay topologies mapped cleanly onto SD-WAN.

SD-WAN policies

SLA and steering, traffic conditioning, and application-aware routing.

Security policies & rules

Firewall rules, NAT, and zones — with shadow and redundancy cleanup along the way.

Services

Custom TCP/UDP and application service definitions, translated faithfully.

Objects

Address objects, groups, and FQDNs — normalized and de-duplicated, not blindly copied.

Many vendors in, one platform out

How it works

Supervised porting, not blind conversion

Source config is parsed into a vendor-neutral intermediate model, translated to the target platform, and reviewed by an expert before anything touches production.

AI accelerates the heavy lifting; deterministic logic and human review own correctness. That distinction — supervised porting rather than blind auto-conversion — is what keeps networks up during a migration.

The differentiator

Validation that outlasts the project

After cutover, lightweight software agents regularly exercise the migrated policies and report pass / fail against intent — catching drift, regressions, and silent breakage long after the project closes.
