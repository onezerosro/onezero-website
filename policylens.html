The problem

Security-policy sprawl is invisible — until it isn't

Enterprises running dozens to hundreds of branch sites accumulate security-policy debt: rules drift between sites, shadowed and overly-permissive rules pile up, address objects go orphaned, and no one can prove the estate is consistent or compliant.

Today this is audited by hand by senior engineers — slow, expensive, and stale the moment it's finished. NIS2 and DORA now turn that gap into a legal and board-level liability.

Use cases

What PolicyLens finds — and proves

Two real-world scenarios where continuous policy intelligence makes the difference between a clean audit and a compliance incident.

1

Catch what migration missed — and what an engineer changed overnight

When platforms are cut over, rules are complex and translators aren't perfect. PolicyLens continuously compares source and target policy sets — and flags any manual change made outside the change window.

  • Migration gap detected: a critical deny rule for RDP traffic existed on the source platform but was not ported to the target — silently leaving an exposure.
  • Unauthorized manual edit: an engineer expanded a destination from a subnet to ANY at 08:47 — outside any approved change window and not logged in the ITSM.
  • Immediate alert, attributed finding: PolicyLens surfaces both within minutes of the next config pull, with the rule ID, site, and the exact delta — ready to attach to an incident ticket.

2

Daily policy report — stored, attributable, auditable

An enterprise can't prove NIS2 or DORA compliance with spot-checks. PolicyLens generates a timestamped, attributable daily record of every rule and object change across the entire estate — stored automatically in the Audit Room.

  • Complete change log, by person: every addition, modification, and deletion is recorded with the engineer's identity, site, and exact timestamp — no manual log required.
  • Baseline snapshot included: the report captures not just what changed but the full current state — so an auditor can compare any day against any prior day.
  • Stored as evidence: each report is written to the Audit Room evidence store, date-stamped and tamper-evident — attachable directly to a NIS2 Art. 21 or DORA Art. 11 control response.
